Rewiring Wellness, One Community at a Time

Grounded in neuroscience, powered by community

Read, Write, and Run (RWR) is a nonprofit collective dedicated to transforming mental health and wellness through an evidence-based, multi-modal approach. We believe that engaging with fine literature, practicing creative writing, and participating in regular physical activity can literally rewire our brains for greater resilience and reduced stress.

Founded on principles from neuroscience and psychiatry, RWR creates accessible, community-based programs that combine the therapeutic benefits of arts engagement with the proven mental health benefits of exercise. Our approach addresses the growing mental health crisis by providing low-barrier, high-impact interventions that foster both individual wellness and community connection.

Our Programs

Accessible pathways to wellness for diverse communities

Community Cohorts

8-12 week programs combining guided reading discussions, creative writing workshops, and group runs or walks. Build accountability, social support, and lasting habits.

  • Weekly meetings
  • Structured curriculum
  • Peer support
  • Measured outcomes

Workplace Wellness

Partner with organizations to offer stress reduction programs for employees. Lunchtime book discussions, writing sessions, and after-work run clubs designed to combat burnout.

  • Customizable format
  • On-site or hybrid
  • ROI tracking
  • Scalable model

Youth Programs

Age-appropriate programming for adolescents facing unprecedented mental health challenges. School-based and after-school partnerships that help young people build resilience.

  • Adapted curriculum
  • Shorter sessions
  • Engaging content
  • Development-focused

Clinical Integration

Evidence-based adjunct programming for community mental health centers. Complement traditional treatment with structured wellness interventions and collect outcome data.

  • Provider partnerships
  • Validated measures
  • Research protocols
  • Grant-funded
